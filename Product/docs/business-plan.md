## 1. Business Overview

**Flow AI** is a vertical AI agent platform that automates customer conversations for service-industry SMEs and mid-market businesses. The platform captures structured data from real customer interactions — across WhatsApp, website chat, and other messaging touchpoints — enabling businesses to respond 24/7, qualify leads automatically, and build institutional knowledge from every interaction.

### Target Markets
Southeast Asia — primarily Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia (premium segment), Philippines — where WhatsApp and chat-first communication is the default B2C channel and service businesses operate with lean teams.

### Business Model
- **Phase 1 (Now):** Custom AI agent builds for anchor clients — generating revenue while validating product-market fit
- **Phase 2:** Verticalized SaaS platform built on learnings from Phase 1
- **Phase 3:** Platform + data intelligence layer with benchmarking and analytics as premium add-ons

---

## 2. The Problem

Service businesses in SEA share a universal pain cluster:

| Problem | Business Impact |
|---|---|
| Inquiries come in 24/7 across WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, and website — staff can only respond during hours | Lost leads, slow response = lost jobs and revenue |
| Same questions answered manually by every staff member | Inconsistent quoting, wasted labor, poor customer experience |
| No structured record of what customers asked, wanted, or paid | Zero business intelligence, no pipeline visibility |
| Scaling customer service means hiring headcount | High operational cost, training burden |
| Follow-ups (reminders, re-engagement, renewals) done manually or not at all | Poor retention and low customer lifetime value |

**The core insight:** These businesses are drowning in unstructured conversational data and have no system to capture, structure, or act on it. Every conversation that ends without a booking is invisible lost revenue.

## 3. Unique Value Proposition

> **"Flow AI turns your customer conversations into a 24/7 automated sales and service system — so your team only handles what truly needs a human."**

### Three Layers of Value

**Layer 1 — Automated Response & Lead Qualification**
AI handles first-touch across WhatsApp and website chat: greets leads, answers FAQs, collects job/service details, qualifies intent and budget, and routes to the right human when needed.

**Layer 2 — Structured Conversation Data Capture**
Every conversation is parsed into structured fields: service type, customer intent, budget signals, decision timeline, and outcome. This is a business intelligence asset the client never had before.

**Layer 3 — Compounding Service Intelligence**
The longer a business runs on Flow AI, the more the system learns their pricing patterns, common objections, peak demand periods, and conversion rates — creating a personalized AI agent that improves over time.

## 4. Target Client Segments

### Prioritization Criteria
Target clients with **high inquiry volume** AND **high revenue per customer or high transaction frequency** AND **clear willingness to pay for operational efficiency**.

### Tier 1 — Primary Targets

**Aesthetics, Beauty & Wellness Clinics**
- High inbound inquiry volume across WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, and website simultaneously
- LTV per customer is $500–$5,000+; treatment bookings, pricing questions, follow-up appointments are all conversation-heavy
- Staff spend hours on repetitive inquiries ("how much is a facial?", "any slots this Friday?") instead of serving clients
- Healthy margins; already spend on booking systems and POS software
- Franchise and chain operators give multi-location expansion built-in
- **Willingness to pay: High**

**Real Estate Agencies & Property Developers**
- Extremely high inquiry-to-close conversion pressure; agents buried in leads that need qualification
- High-ticket transactions mean capturing 1–2 more deals per month easily justifies software spend
- AI qualification (budget, timeline, location preference, buyer vs renter) before assigning an agent has clear ROI
- Accustomed to paying for lead gen and CRM tools
- **Willingness to pay: Very High**

**Insurance Brokers & Independent Financial Advisors**
- Lead qualification and follow-up cadence is core to the business
- Compliance-safe automation of reminders (policy renewal, annual review) is high value
- Hundreds of prospects at different pipeline stages — impossible to manage manually at scale
- Commission-based income means conversion tools are easy to justify
- **Willingness to pay: High**

**Education — Enrichment Centres, Tuition Franchises**
- High inquiry volume from parents (trial class questions, scheduling, fees, curriculum)
- Enrollment cycles are repeatable and predictable
- Admin-heavy — same questions asked by every new parent; scheduling, trial booking, payment confirmation
- Franchise operators have IT budgets and multi-location needs
- **Willingness to pay: Medium–High**

**Mid-size Renovation & Interior Design Firms**
- High-ticket jobs ($30K–$500K+) with many tire-kickers; qualifying budget and timeline is critical before investing designer time
- Designers currently waste hours on consultations that don't convert because prospect had a $10K budget for a $100K job
- Structured intake qualification has direct, measurable ROI
- **Willingness to pay: High per project; recurring SaaS is a stretch — price accordingly**

**Healthcare — Dental Chains, GP Clinics, Specialist Clinics**
- Appointment volume is high; no-show rates are costly; follow-up reminders reduce revenue leakage
- Receptionists handling repetitive appointment/price/availability questions on loop
- Multi-branch clinic groups have IT budgets and process standardization needs
- AI-assist model (human still in clinical loop) fits regulatory requirements
- **Willingness to pay: High for chains and group practices**

### Tier 2 — Secondary Targets (Phase 2+)
- Car dealerships and premium service workshops
- Hotel and hospitality (concierge and booking automation)
- Legal firms (initial inquiry triage and intake)
- Moving and logistics companies

### Who to Deprioritize
- Solo operators and micro-businesses (single-person repair shops, solo cleaners)
- Price-sensitive markets without established digital infrastructure
- Businesses with fewer than ~50 inbound inquiries per month (ROI doesn't justify the cost)

## 6. Competitive Moat

### Primary Moat — Vertical Data Intelligence
Generic platforms handle conversation routing. Flow AI captures **service-industry-specific structured data at scale** — service types, pricing ranges, geographic demand, objection patterns, conversion rates by vertical.

Over time this creates:
- Better AI responses tuned to service language (e.g., "chemical wash", "hacking work", "full set veneer", "cash buyer")
- Benchmarking data across similar businesses in the same vertical
- Training data that generic competitors cannot replicate without the same vertical focus

### Secondary Moat — Workflow Integration Depth
Deep integrations into tools service businesses already use:
- Google Business Profile (click-to-WhatsApp/chat triggers)
- Booking and scheduling systems (aesthetic clinic PMS, clinic software)
- CRM and pipeline tools (for real estate and insurance verticals)
- Invoicing and payment (for renovation and service verticals)

Each integration raises switching cost and widens the gap from generic tools.

### Tertiary Moat — Vertical Reputation & Referral Networks
Service businesses refer within tight industry guilds and associations. One high-profile customer in a vertical (e.g., a well-known aesthetics chain) creates a referral flywheel. Vertical reputation compounds faster than horizontal reputation.

## 7. Product & Service Strategy

### The Recommended Path: Vertical SaaS with Professional Services Wrapper

Do not build a generic platform. Do not run a pure custom dev agency. Build a **purpose-built SaaS product** with a **professional services tier** for clients who need bespoke work — using custom builds in Phase 1 to fund and inform the SaaS product in Phase 2.

### Phase 1 — Custom Builds (Now)
Take on anchor clients as custom AI agent projects. Use this phase to:
- Generate early revenue to fund product development
- Validate which workflows, verticals, and integrations matter most
- Build case studies and reference customers
- Understand the real operational pain points before building a self-serve product

**Deliverable to client:** A fully configured AI agent (WhatsApp + website chat) built on proven infrastructure (n8n, WhatsApp API, LLM layer). Client gets a working system. Flow AI gets paid and retains learnings.

**Key discipline:** Document every build. Every custom configuration that gets reused is a future SaaS feature. Every FAQ template, intake form, or escalation rule that works across clients becomes a vertical preset.

### Phase 2 — Verticalized SaaS Product
Launch the SaaS platform pre-configured for 1–2 verticals. The product includes:
- AI conversation agent deployable on WhatsApp AND website landing page (same engine, multiple surfaces)
- Vertical-specific preset: treatment categories, intake flows, FAQ templates, pricing range handling — ready on day one
- Structured data capture dashboard: every conversation logged as intent, service type, budget signal, outcome
- Follow-up automation: appointment reminders, re-engagement sequences, renewal nudges
- Human handoff: live agent escalation with full conversation context passed through
- Analytics: response rate, conversion rate, inquiry volume trends

### Phase 3 — Platform + Data Layer
- Benchmarking and market insight reports (what are similar clinics charging? what is the average conversion rate for renovation leads?)
- API access for enterprise clients to pipe data into their own systems
- Marketplace or referral network as a future revenue stream

### What to Build On
Use **n8n, WhatsApp Business API, and an LLM layer** as the underlying infrastructure. Clients should never see the stack — they should see "Flow AI for Aesthetics Clinics." The IP is in the vertical configuration, trained workflows, and data model — not the infrastructure.

### Revenue Streams

| Stream | Description | Phase |
|---|---|---|
| Custom build fee | One-time project fee for bespoke AI agent builds | Phase 1 |
| Monthly retainer | Ongoing support, maintenance, and iteration on custom builds | Phase 1 |
| SaaS subscription | Monthly recurring for the self-serve platform | Phase 2+ |
| Setup & onboarding fee | One-time fee to configure AI persona and integrate channels | Phase 2+ |
| Enterprise tier | Custom persona, API access, CRM integrations, advanced analytics | Phase 2+ |
| Professional services | Custom workflow builds for enterprise clients needing bespoke automation | All phases |

## 8. Pricing Strategy

### Philosophy
**Earn trust first, then earn scale.** Phase 1 pricing is intentionally relationship-driven — price to get in the door, deliver undeniable value, then formalize into a recurring model. Do not try to launch with a rigid pricing sheet before you have validated the product.

---

### Phase 1 — Custom Build Pricing (Current)

This is a **bespoke development and services model**. Price based on scope, not a rate card.

| Engagement Type | Price Range (SGD) | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Starter Custom Build | $2,500 – $5,000 one-time | WhatsApp or website AI agent, up to 2 workflow types (e.g., inquiry + booking), 1 channel, 30-day support |
| Full Custom Build | $5,000 – $15,000 one-time | Multi-channel (WhatsApp + website), full intake and qualification flows, CRM integration, 60-day support |
| Monthly Retainer (post-build) | $500 – $1,500/month | Ongoing maintenance, prompt tuning, new FAQ additions, conversation analytics review |

**Pricing philosophy for Phase 1:**
- First 3–5 clients: consider discounting 30–50% in exchange for a detailed case study, referral rights, and logo usage
- Do not undercharge to the point of devaluing the work — $2,500 minimum signals professionalism
- Retainer is critical: it converts a one-time project into recurring revenue and keeps you close to the client's evolving needs
- Bundle a 3-month retainer into the initial build price to establish the habit

---

### Phase 2 — SaaS Subscription Pricing (Platform Launch)

Tiered SaaS with a setup fee to cover onboarding effort.

| Tier | Target Client | Monthly Price (SGD) | Setup Fee | Key Inclusions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | Single-location SME | $149/month | $499 one-time | 1 channel (WhatsApp or website), up to 1,000 conversations/month, basic analytics, standard FAQ templates |
| **Growth** | Multi-staff, single location | $349/month | $999 one-time | 2 channels, unlimited conversations, lead pipeline dashboard, follow-up automation, human handoff |
| **Pro** | Multi-location, franchise | $799–$1,499/month | $2,000+ | Custom AI persona, multi-location management, API access, CRM integration, priority support |
| **Enterprise** | Large chain, developer | Custom | Custom | Full white-label, custom integrations, dedicated support, SLA |

**Pricing rationale:**
- $149 is accessible for a clinic or education centre doing $20K+/month in revenue — less than 1% of revenue
- $349 for Growth makes the ROI argument easy: one additional booking per month typically covers it
- Setup fees ensure onboarding is taken seriously and offset the cost of doing it well
- Annual billing option at 2 months free (incentivizes commitment, improves cash flow)

---

### Phase 1 → Phase 2 Transition Strategy

When a custom-build client is ready to move to SaaS:
- Migrate them to the equivalent SaaS tier and reduce their retainer to the platform fee
- Position it as an upgrade, not a change: "everything we built for you is now on our platform with more features"
- Offer a grandfathered rate for early clients (e.g., Growth tier at $249/month for first 12 months)
- This transition also validates that the SaaS product handles what was previously custom — the best form of QA

---

### Pricing Guardrails

- **Never price purely on cost.** Price on value delivered (time saved, leads captured, revenue unlocked).
- **Never discount the retainer.** The monthly relationship is where learning and retention happen.
- **Charge a setup fee always.** Free onboarding signals low value and attracts the wrong clients.
- **Revisit pricing after every 5 clients.** Early pricing is a hypothesis. Raise prices as you accumulate case studies and confidence.

## 9. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1 — Nail One Vertical (Months 1–6)

**Recommended starting vertical: Aesthetics & Wellness Clinics**

Why this vertical first:
- High inquiry volume = visible, immediate ROI from automation
- High LTV = willingness to pay is strong
- Franchise operators = land one, expand to all locations
- Conversation patterns are repetitive and well-defined = easier to train AI on
- Strong word-of-mouth referral culture within the industry

**Target:** 5–10 paying clients in this vertical. Not for scale — for proof, case studies, and referrals.

### Phase 2 — Expand Horizontally (Months 7–18)

Use the playbook from aesthetics to enter real estate, insurance, and education. Each vertical gets a pre-configured AI persona with pre-loaded FAQ templates, intake flows, and vertical-specific data taxonomy.

### Phase 3 — Platform + Partnerships (Month 18+)

- Self-serve SaaS with inbound acquisition
- Channel partnerships with clinic management software, real estate CRMs, insurance platforms
- Benchmarking reports and data products as premium add-ons

## 11. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| WhatsApp Business API policy changes (Meta) | Build an abstraction layer; support website chat as parallel channel; maintain direct API relationships; diversify channel integrations |
| Low tech adoption among target clients | Invest in white-glove onboarding; Phase 1 custom builds reduce adoption friction entirely |
| Generic AI tools commoditize the space | Double down on vertical data and workflow integrations — generic tools will not go deep in specific service verticals |
| respond.io / WATI add AI features | Vertical depth and data moat is the answer; horizontal platforms cannot match vertical-specific configuration and intelligence |
| Phase 1 custom builds delay SaaS product | Strict discipline: every custom build must produce a reusable component or vertical template. Time-box custom engagements. |
| Client dependency on bespoke builds | Phase 1 retainers are a bridge, not a destination. Set internal deadline for SaaS launch and communicate roadmap to clients. |

## 12. Strategic Thesis

Most service SMEs in SEA are running their entire customer acquisition and service pipeline through informal conversations — across WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, and website forms — with zero structure, zero data, and zero leverage.

**Flow AI is the vertical AI layer that turns that conversational chaos into a system.**

The path:
1. **Start narrow** — custom builds for anchor clients in one vertical, generating revenue and validation simultaneously
2. **Go deep before going wide** — master the aesthetics/wellness vertical before expanding; become the obvious choice in that category
3. **Productize what works** — every custom build is R&D for the SaaS platform; ship the platform when patterns are clear
4. **Own the data layer** — the moat is not the chatbot interface; it is the structured, vertical-specific conversation data that compounds in value over time and cannot be replicated by horizontal platforms

The moat is not built in one day. It is built one vertical, one client, one conversation at a time.
